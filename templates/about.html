About

This website aims to show how different cinematic traits perfom in the Bechdel test. The test is a vey simple way to check how or even if female characters are participating in a film. The bash is on. Check out if your favorite writer has a higher score than the average production from a certain country, gerne or director.

As a very simple measurement, a higher score indicates a higher female participation in movie. It only needs one conversation of two named female characters, that is not about a male character to achieve the full score of three points. Yet, not even 58% of all rated movies in the databank have the maximum score for this easy task.

Scoring

Every movie in the database is rated with 0, 1, 2 or 3 points. To compare the overall performance of a certain cinematic trait, an average over all respective movies is calculated. Two movies with 0 and one movie with 3 point, would result in an average of 1.0 point. This scoring system is based on the original Bechdel test.

Origin of the Bechdel test

The rules now known as the Bechdel test first appeared in 1985 in Alison Bechdel's comic strip Dykes To Watch Out For. In a strip titled "The Rule", two women discuss seeing a film and one woman explains that she only goes to a movie if it satisfies the following requirements:

  1. The movie has to have at least two women in it,
  2. who talk to each other,
  3. about something besides a man

Origin of the Data

The ratings are taken from the API of bechdeltest.com. Additional information like cast, genre or country of origin are taken from the IMDb. The database is updated once a day to check for new ratings on bechdeltest.com.
